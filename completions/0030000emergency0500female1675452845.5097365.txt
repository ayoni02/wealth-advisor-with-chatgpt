Given this information, our advice would be as follows:

Build up your emergency fund:

The first thing you should do is build up your emergency fund. An emergency fund is a savings account that you can use in case of unexpected expenses, such as a job loss or medical emergency. We recommend saving enough to cover three to six months of living expenses.

Save for retirement:

The next thing you should do is start saving for retirement. Retirement may seem like a long way off, but the sooner you start saving, the better. We recommend saving 10-15% of your income for retirement.

Reduce your monthly expenses:

One way to free up more money to save is to reduce your monthly expenses. A good place to start is by evaluating your monthly expenses and seeing if there are any areas where you can cut back. For example, you may be able to save money by cooking at home more often or reducing your cable bill.

 Invest your money:

Another way to grow your savings is to invest your money. Investing allows you to grow your money while taking less risk than gambling or speculative activities. We recommend investing in a mix of stocks, bonds, and mutual funds.
