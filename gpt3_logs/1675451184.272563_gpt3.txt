The first and most important thing this person should do is to build up an emergency fund. This fund should be able to cover at least three months of living expenses in case of a job loss or other unforeseen circumstance. Once the emergency fund is established, the person can begin to focus on other financial goals.

It is also important for this person to make sure that their income is consistently exceeding their monthly expenses. If their income is not enough to cover their expenses, they will need to find ways to either reduce their expenses or increase their income. One option for increasing income could be to look for a higher paying job. Another option could be to generate additional income through side hustles or investments.

This person should also make sure to take advantage of any employer matching programs for retirement savings. If their employer offers a 401(k) match, they should make sure to contribute enough to receive the full match. Additionally, they should try to max out their contribution to the 401(k) each year.
