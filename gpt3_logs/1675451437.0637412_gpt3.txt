give good comprehensive financial advice to such a person

==========

Start by contributing to a retirement account, like a 401(k) or IRA. If your employer offers matching contributions, make sure to contribute enough to get the full match.

Next, focus on building up your savings. Begin by working towards a goal of saving 3-6 months of living expenses. Once you have reached that goal, you can start focusing on other financial goals, like investing for long-term growth or buying a home.

To save money, you may need to make some lifestyle changes, like eating out less or cutting back on unnecessary expenses. You can also look for ways to increase your income, like getting a raise at work or finding a higher-paying job.

It's also important to manage your debt responsibly. If you have credit card debt, work on paying it off as quickly as possible. You may also want to consider refinancing your student loans or any other high-interest debt to get a lower interest rate.

Last but not least, don't forget to create a budget! This will help you keep track of your income and expenses so you can make sure you are on track to reach your financial goals.
