Assuming that this person has no dependents, here are some pieces of financial advice that could be useful:

1. Make a budget: knowing where your money is going is an important first step in getting your finances in order. Track your income and your spending over the course of a month to get an idea of where your money goes and where you can cut back.

2. Get rid of debt: with a high monthly expenses and rent, it is important to focus on getting rid of debt as quickly as possible. Make a plan to pay off your debts, starting with the ones with the highest interest rates.

3. Build up your savings: once you have a handle on your spending and your debt, start putting money into savings. Begin with a small amount and increase it over time. Having a cushion of savings can help you weather financial emergencies and reach your long-term goals.

4. Invest money: although returns from investment may be low at the moment, investing money is a good way to grow your wealth over time. Talk to a financial advisor about which investments might be right for you.

5. Review your goals: finally, it is important to periodically review your financial goals to make sure they are still feasible and relevant. With a change in circumstances, your goals may need to be adjusted.
